THE FATHER OF MODERN FANTASY

Tolkien, in 1966.

DISCOVER

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien, internationally known as J. R. R. Tolkien, is widely considered one of the greatest authors of fantasy literature. Often hailed as the "father of modern fantasy," his profound influence on the genre is indisputable. Tolkien's works, including "The Hobbit," "The Lord of the Rings," and "The Silmarillion," introduced readers to meticulously crafted worlds filled with rich histories, languages, and mythologies.

His groundbreaking approach to world-building and storytelling set a standard for generations of fantasy writers, inspiring them to create immersive fictional universes.
